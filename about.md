# Aravena

ARAVENA moves through the world wrapped in layers of dark fabric, as if the act of being seen is something that must always be managed. Her face is that of a raven, and it is the first thing people register, sharp and immediate, like recognition arriving too fast to be trusted. The rest of her body is human, tinted deep blue, moving quietly beneath the weight of concealment.
She does not often stop in places where light is clear. When she does, people tend to look twice, then look away, unsure of what exactly they saw the first time. Her presence never holds a single definition for long.
From her back, six spider-like limbs extend and shift with careful coordination, not chaotic, but deliberate, as if they are part of an internal system that keeps her from falling out of alignment. They move with her natural body, not against it, giving her motion a layered, unfamiliar rhythm.
There are moments when she passes through crowds and feels nothing break on the surface, and other moments when something in her face is read too quickly, too sharply, and the world around her becomes briefly unstable, like attention itself has cut too deep.
Still, she continues forward. Not becoming something new, not returning to something old, but remaining in motion while all parts of her hold their place at once, slowly learning how to stop resisting each other.
And over time, what separates her form begins to quiet, not through change, but through recognition settling into something stable enough to remain.
